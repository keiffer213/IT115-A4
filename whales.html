Whales

Humpback Whales

  • Length: Up to 52 ft
  • Weight: Up to 60,000 lbs
  • Lifespan: Up to 50 years
  • Diet: ???
  • Threats: Entanglement in fishing gear, vessel-based harassment, and underwater noise
  • Distribution: Humpbacks travel in all oceans around the world and have the longest migration patterns
  • Conservation Status: Listed under the endangered species act of 1970 and 1973
  • Sightings in the Pacific Northwest: any reported sightings around the Alki Beach area and up further north in British Columbia.

Grey Whales

  • Length: Up to 49 ft
  • Weight: Up to 90,000 lbs
  • Lifespan: Up to 60 years
  • Diet: Eats up to 2,400 pounds of food a day, including squid, shrimp, and schools of small fish
  • Threats: Oil and gas development, fishing gear, and large ships
  • Distribution: Northern Hemisphere, Northern Pacific Ocean
  • Conservation Status: Protected under the Marine Mammal Act, and District Population Segment (DPS). Was once listed under the endangered species act but was unlisted in 1994
  • Sightings in the Pacific Northwest: ???

Minke Whale

  • Length: Up to 35 ft
  • Weight: Up to 20,000 lbs
  • Lifespan: Up to 50 years
  • Diet: Krill and small fish
  • Threats: Climate change, vessels, and underground sea noise
  • Distribution: North Pacific Ocean and Bering Sea
  • Conservation Status: Covered under the Marine Mammal Act
  • Sightings in the Pacific Northwest: Puget Sound and San Juan Islands

Orca Whale

  • Length: Up to 32 ft
  • Weight: Up to 22,000 lbs
  • Lifespan: Up to 90 years
  • Diet: Fish and squid
  • Threats: Food limitations, vessels and vessel disturbances, and chemical contaminants
  • Distribution: North Pacific Ocean and Bering Sea
  • Conservation Status: Protected under the MMPA and the Southern resident population is protected under the ESA and soon to be considered not an endangered species.
  • Sightings in the Pacific Northwest: All over the Puget Sound region up to as far as the San Juan Islands and British Colombia
